Protons, neutrons, and electrons are all (very important) subatomic particles, which together compose an atom. Protons and neutrons1 form the nucleus, whereas electrons orbit around said nucleus.

The proton has an atomic mass of 1.00759u and are positively charged. and neutrons have a mass of 1.00848u and they do not have a charge. Electrons have an indiscernible mass, and a negative charge2 (see also: [[Relative Atomic Mass]]).
