[!IMPORTANT] Equations of Motion Find them here: [[Equations of Motion]]

  1. This one:

    \[\begin{align} \text{To prove: } v&=u+at \\ \text{Proof: } a&=\frac{\Delta v}{t} \\ a&=\frac{v-u}{t} \\ at&=v-u \\ \text{hence }v&=u+at \end{align}\]

  2. This other one:

    \[\begin{align} \text{To prove: } s&=\frac{1}{2}at^{2}+ut\\ \text{Proof: }v_{av}&=\frac{v+u}{2} \\ \text{but }v_{av}&=\frac{s}{t}\\ \therefore \frac{s}{t}&=\frac{v+u}{2} \\ \text{but }v&=at+u \\ \therefore \frac{s}{t}&=\frac{at+u+u}{2} \text{ QED} \\ \frac{s}{t}&=\frac{at+2u}{2} \\ \frac{s}{t}&=\frac{1}{2}at+u \\ \text{hence }s&=\frac{1}{2}at^{2}+ut \end{align}\]

  3. That other one:

\[\begin{align} \text{To prove: } v^{2}&=u^{2}+2as \\ \text{Proof: } v&=u+at \\ v^{2}&=(u+at)^{2} \\ v^{2}&=u^{2}+2uat+a^{2}t^{2} \\ v^{2}&=u^{2}+2a\left( ut+\frac{1}{2}at^{2} \right) \\ \text{but } s&=\frac{1}{2}at^{2}+ut \\ \text{hence } v^{2}&=u^{2}+2as \end{align}\]
