Schrodinger used probability measures for shells (see also [[Schrodinger Shell Model]]). This eventually culminated in orbitals and subshells. Orbitals are a way of predicting where an electron may be within an electron cloud1. There are 4 kinds, s orbitals, p, d, and f. Here is my crappy rendition:

![[Bad Orbitals.png]]

You should probably search it up. Anyways, orbitals are a subshell. There are many orbitals in one shell.

Shell Orbitals
K (one) 1s
L (one) 2s (three) 2p
M (one) 3s (three) 3p (five) 3d
N (one) 4s (three) 4p (five) 4d (seven) 4f

Going back to the magnesium example (see [[Shell Model]]), the orbitals would be: $1s^{2}2s^{2}2p^{6}3s^{2}$.
