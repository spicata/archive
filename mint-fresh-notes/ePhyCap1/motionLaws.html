There are three laws of motion:

  1. An object of mass will remain at rest, or maintain a constant velocity unless an unbalanced force acts upon it.
  2. Force is equal to the acceleration times the mass of an object.
  3. When two objects exert a force on each other, these forces are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction.
